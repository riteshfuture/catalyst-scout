### 🧠 Scoring Logic Breakdown

The agent utilizes a two-phase evaluation logic to score and rank candidates, mimicking a human recruiter's decision-making process while optimizing for compute efficiency.

**1. The Domain Filter (Pass/Fail):**
Before any scoring occurs, the agent evaluates the Job Description (JD) to categorize its domain (Tech vs. Non-Tech). It uses Python `set()` intersections to instantly eliminate candidates outside the domain, ensuring the AI only scores relevant talent.

**2. The Dual-Score Evaluation:**
Relevant candidates are passed to the Gemini LLM (or the local fallback engine) to generate two distinct metrics:
* **Match Score (0-100%):** The LLM strictly evaluates the overlap between the JD requirements and the candidate's Title, Skills, and Years of Experience. Penalties are applied for seniority mismatches (e.g., a junior applying for a senior role).
* **Interest Score (0-100%):** The LLM acts as the candidate, reading their hidden "vibe" (e.g., active, passive, desperate) to simulate a realistic email reply to a recruiter outreach, assigning a percentage based on their likelihood to engage.

**3. The Ranking Algorithm:**
The final Pandas DataFrame sorts the shortlist primarily by the **Match Score** (descending). If two candidates are equally qualified, the **Interest Score** acts as the tie-breaker, pushing highly motivated candidates to the top.
